# PM / Training Lead
**Remote (US / Europe) · Contract · 6 months · 50% allocation**

---

Ventoux AI builds AI-powered decision intelligence for healthcare supply chains. Our platform turns fragmented procurement data, demand signals, and supplier performance into actionable insight — helping supply chain teams anticipate problems before they become stockouts. We work with organizations responsible for delivering essential medicines to hundreds of millions of people across Sub-Saharan Africa.

We're a small team tackling one of global health's hardest operational problems: making sure the right medicines reach the right places at the right time. Our technology is already working. Now we're deploying it within one of the world's most important supply chain operations — and we need someone who can bridge the gap between our engineering team and the people who will use the platform daily.

## The Mission

We're partnering with a major UN agency and one of the world's largest philanthropic foundations to modernize supply chain decision-making across 100+ countries. Our client procures and distributes essential health commodities globally — reproductive health medicines, contraceptives, and life-saving supplies. Their procurement teams are experienced, capable professionals — but their tools haven't kept up with the complexity of their operations.

We're building an AI-powered intelligence layer that changes that. But technology alone doesn't create change — adoption does. Your job is to make sure Ventoux doesn't just work technically, but works *for the people who use it*. That means managing delivery, designing training, facilitating workshops, and translating user feedback into product improvements.

The work matters. These supply chains prevent maternal deaths, treat HIV, and provide family planning to some of the most underserved communities on earth. When the tools work and people trust them, better decisions follow.

## Our Team

We're small, fast, and technical. You'll be the non-engineering voice on a four-person team — the person who keeps delivery on track and ensures that what we build actually gets adopted. You'll work alongside a full-stack engineer, a data engineer, and the founder.

You don't need to be technical, but you need to be comfortable around technical people and technical conversations. You'll participate in sprint demos, review deliverables, and translate between engineering priorities and stakeholder expectations.

## Role

This is a hybrid PM + training role. Half your time is project management — coordinating a small distributed team, managing deliverables against milestones, and keeping stakeholders aligned across Ventoux, the foundation, and the UN agency. The other half is designing and delivering training programs that put the platform in the hands of procurement staff.

The engagement has clear milestones (6–8 week interim review, final delivery at month 6) and a defined scope. You'll own the project plan, stakeholder reporting, and risk management. On the training side, you'll design onboarding materials, facilitate workshops (remote and in-person in Copenhagen), and build the knowledge transfer documentation that ensures the client can operate independently after the engagement.

## What You'll Do

- Own the project plan — sprint cadence, milestone tracking, deliverable management, and risk identification
- Manage stakeholder communication across Ventoux, the foundation program officer, and the UN agency's procurement leadership
- Design and deliver training workshops for procurement staff — both remote sessions and in-person workshops in Copenhagen (2–3 trips)
- Create user onboarding materials, guides, and reference documentation tailored to non-technical procurement professionals
- Facilitate user feedback sessions and translate insights into actionable product requirements for the engineering team
- Coordinate sprint demos and progress reviews with client stakeholders
- Build the knowledge transfer package for client handover — ensuring the platform can be operated and maintained independently
- Track engagement health — budget, timeline, scope, and team capacity

## What We Look For

- **5+ years of project management experience** — you've managed delivery for technical products with distributed teams
- **Experience with international organizations** — you understand how UN agencies, NGOs, or foundation-funded programs operate (procurement cycles, decision-making structures, stakeholder dynamics)
- **Workshop facilitation skills** — you've designed and delivered training for non-technical audiences, ideally in cross-cultural settings
- **Stakeholder management across multiple organizations** — you're comfortable navigating the dynamics between a vendor team, a funding organization, and an implementing agency
- **Knowledge transfer and documentation experience** — you've built handover packages that actually work
- **Willingness to travel to Copenhagen** 2–3 times during the engagement for in-person workshops and planning sessions
- **Professional English proficiency** — all stakeholder communication and documentation will be in English
- **Self-directed and async-capable** — at 50% allocation, you need to manage your time effectively and communicate proactively

### Strong Pluses

- Global health supply chain domain knowledge — procurement, logistics, or commodity management
- French language skills (many partner countries are francophone, and some UN agency staff prefer French)
- Experience with AI/ML product rollouts or change management for technology adoption
- Prior work with UNFPA, WHO, PATH, UNICEF, or similar organizations
- Background in capacity building or institutional strengthening programs
- Experience managing engagements where the deliverable includes both software and training/adoption
